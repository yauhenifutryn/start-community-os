## White-Space Analysis

**What clearly exists:**
- Organizer-side analytics (Devpost, Junction, DoraHacks).
- Sponsor engagement tooling (submission review portals, brand-reach case studies).
- Broad-market talent/company signal at internet scale (Harmonic, Specter), sourced from the whole web, not a community's structured event/applicant data.
- Community-to-hiring marketplaces (Pallet), monetized via placement, not evidence.
- Generic community CRM/analytics (Common Room; Orbit and Commsor dead), built for GTM signal, and a cautionary tale about the standalone community-analytics SaaS category.

**What genuinely does not exist (as surfaced by this research):**
- Nobody ingests a single community's structured event/application data (Luma + Devpost), normalizes and anonymizes it, and packages it as a polished static partner-facing artifact showing the full talent funnel + demographics + cross-event trends.
- Nobody frames sponsor-ROI evidence AND VC deal-flow evidence in the same artifact from the same community dataset.
- Nobody positions this as sellable infrastructure for many communities. Pallet is the nearest analog but sells transactions, not evidence, and does not productize anonymization as a trust mechanism.
- "Data clean room" is established in adtech; zero uses found of that pattern applied to community talent reporting.

**Verdict:** not reinventing an existing wheel. The pieces exist separately in adjacent categories; the combination and the static-shareable-artifact business model appear genuinely open. The real risk is category durability, not novelty: the two nearest categories have not proven durable standalone businesses. Sell the report, not the platform, at least initially.

## Positioning Implications

- Borrow VC-native vocabulary: "talent signal", "talent density", "deal flow", "traction". It resonates immediately with the buyer.
- "Data room" is owned by fundraising doc-sharing (DocSend, Carta). "Talent data room" does not conflict but expect brief confusion; lead with the anonymized-funnel visual, not a document repository.
- "Community OS" appears unclaimed as a branded term.
- Devpost's free-Metrics vs paid-Managed-Services split is a pricing precedent: raw analytics are commoditized, the polished narrative report is the premium layer. Matches the plan: the data room is the sellable artifact.
- Given Orbit/Commsor deaths: the wedge is the sponsor/VC-facing deliverable, not "another community analytics tool".
